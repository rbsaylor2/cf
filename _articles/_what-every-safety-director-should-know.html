Overall Safety is vital to a successful business operation

While this white paper provides an easy-to-use guide to help determine the right footwear for a specific work environment, we must first talk broadly about the priority of instituting proper workplace safety regulations, procedures and practices. Whether your company has five employees or 5,000, workplace safety is vital for protecting the individual as well as ensuring that your business is compliant with industry standards set forth by OSHA (Occupational Safety & Health Administration) within the U.S. Department of Labor.

The Occupational Safety and Health Act passed in 1970 established OSHA with the mission of “assuring safe and healthful working conditions for working men and women by setting and enforcing standards and by providing training, outreach, education and assistance”1 and covers most private sector employers in all 50 states, the District of Columbia, and other U.S., jurisdictions. It’s General Duty Clause requires an employer to comply with the Act and regulations derived from it, and to provide employees with “employment and a place of employment which are free from recognized hazards that are causing or are likely to cause death or serious physical harm to his employees.”
